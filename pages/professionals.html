For Professionals

Protective action, clear process, human language

This page supports educators and professionals with general guidance and tools. It is not legal advice. Always follow your local laws, organizational policy, and training.

If you believe a child is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services now. For non emergency concerns, follow your mandated reporting protocol.

Mandated reporting overview (general)

In many regions, certain professionals are required to report reasonable suspicion of child abuse or neglect. “Reasonable suspicion” often means you have a concern based on what you observed or what was disclosed.

  • Your role is not to investigate. It is to report and connect to the correct channel.
  • Stay calm and supportive. Your reaction affects safety and disclosure.
  • Document responsibly. Note time, date, observable facts, and exact quotes when appropriate.
  • Share only with those who must know. Confidentiality and privacy reduce harm.

What to say in the moment

If a student discloses something concerning, keep it simple and protective. Avoid shock. Avoid promises you can’t keep.

Supportive phrases

  • “Thank you for telling me.”
  • “I believe you.”
  • “You are not in trouble.”
  • “My job is to help keep you safe.”
  • “I will share only with people whose job is to protect you.”
